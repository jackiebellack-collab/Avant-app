The edit — 3 brands right now
Auralee
auralee.jp · Available at Ssense

Japanese label doing the most considered basics in the market right now. The fabric quality at this price point doesn't exist anywhere else. If you're building an investment wardrobe, start here. The luster plaited knit is the piece.

Totême
toteme-studio.com · Nordstrom

The Swedish brand that figured out how to make a silk shirt that actually fits. The new season has three pieces I'd buy immediately — the charmeuse in ivory, the wide-leg trouser, and a scarf that works harder than any accessory I own.

Baserange
baserange.net

The wildcard this week. A Danish label making clothes out of bamboo and organic cotton that feel like nothing else. Slightly under the radar still — which is exactly when to pay attention.

Creator worth watching
@lucywilliams02 Instagram · 700K

What she's doing right that most creators miss: she treats her feed as an edit, not a feed. Every post connects to the next. The result is an aesthetic that's instantly recognizable from the first image. Brands pay a premium for that kind of visual consistency and audiences reward it with the kind of loyalty that converts. Watch how she introduces new brands — always through the lens of her existing aesthetic, never as a disruption to it.

The signal

Luxury fashion had a difficult year on social in 2025 — flat numbers, declining engagement, audiences fatigued by the same heritage house content. What worked? Smaller brands with emerging creator voices. The lesson isn't that luxury is dead on social, it's that authenticity is finally more valuable than prestige. The brands winning right now are the ones who found creators who genuinely wear their product and let them tell their own story. That gap between brand authority and creator authenticity is where the interesting business is being built.
